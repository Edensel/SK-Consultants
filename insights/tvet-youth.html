The Future of TVET in Africa

Bridging the Skills Gap through Competency-Based Education

Home / Insights / The Future of TVET
Back to Insights
October 24, 2025 Workforce Development Samuel Kablit

Beyond the Classroom

For decades, Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) in Africa has struggled with a perception problem. Often viewed as a second-tier option, it is now emerging as the most critical driver of economic transformation in the region.

The dawn of the Fourth Industrial Revolution requires a workforce that is not just academically qualified but technically competent. This is where Competency-Based Education and Training (CBET) comes into play.

The CBET Shift

Unlike traditional models that focus on rote learning and time spent in class, CBET focuses on outcomes. Can the student weld a joint to international standards? Can they code a mobile application that functions? This shift forces institutions to align closely with the private sector.

"Industry must be in the classroom, and the classroom must be in the industry."

Key Challenges & Opportunities

  • Infrastructure: Many institutions lack modern equipment. Public-Private Partnerships are essential to bridge this gap.
  • Curriculum Relevance: Rapid technological changes mean curricula must be updated dynamically, not decennially.
  • Soft Skills: Employers increasingly value problem-solving and communication as much as technical prowess.

At SK Consultants, we are working with development partners to modernize TVET ecosystems, ensuring that the youth bulge becomes a demographic dividend rather than a ticking time bomb.
