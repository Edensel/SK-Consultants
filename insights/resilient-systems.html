The Fragility of Markets

From the COVID-19 pandemic to climate-induced droughts, recent years have laid bare the fragility of global supply chains. In emerging economies, when specific value chains break, livelihoods are lost instantly.

Resilience is the ability of a market system to absorb shocks and adapt. Building this requires looking beyond the immediate transaction to the supporting functions and rules that govern the market.

The MSD Approach

Market Systems Development (MSD) intervenes at the systemic level. Instead of giving a farmer seeds (a direct intervention), we work with seed companies to build better distribution networks (a systemic intervention).

"If you give a man a fish, you feed him for a day. If you fix the fishing industry, you feed the village forever."

Principles of Resilience

  • Diversity: Avoiding reliance on not just a single buyer or supplier, but a single market channel.
  • Connectivity: Strong relationships between market actors build trust, which is the currency of crisis management.
  • Adaptability: Regulatory frameworks must be flexible enough to allow innovation in response to new challenges.
